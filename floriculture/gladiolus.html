Indian Gladiolus Exporters

Gladiolus, also known as the 'sword lily', has tall flower spikes and large and colourful blooms. The Gladiolus exporter in India exports a huge amount of flowers to various countries in the world. Due to the growing popularity of the Gladiolus flowers, Gladiolus export from India has been rising over the years. The Indian Gladiolus exporter exports increasing amounts of flowers each and every year. The Gladiolus export from India includes the export of several varieties of flowers.

Gladiolus Varieties We Export

The Gladiolus import from India includes many varieties of the product including Archana, Basant Bhar, Gazel, Jwala and so on. The Gladiolus exporter in India exports premium quality products to different countries in the world. The export of Gladiolus includes the following major varieties.

1

Archana

The Archana variety was released by NBRI, Lucknow. This variety is a cross between G. psittacines 'Sylvia' and G. 'Friendship'. The spike of this variety is branched with a mean spike length of around 80cm. The petals of the Archana variety have a central white streak with the throat blotched with primrose yellow.

2

Basant Bhar

The Basant Bhar variety is a seedling produced by selfing G. 'Tunias Challenge'. The mean spike length of this variety is around 50cm. The florets of the Basant Bhar are Empire Yellow with throats speckled magenta. There are around 14 florets/spikes in this variety.

3

Gazel

This variety of Gladiolus was released by NBRI, Lucknow. The Gazel is a seedling produced by selfing G. 'White Friendship'. The mean spike length of this variety is around 35cm. The florets of this variety are Fuchsine pink in colour with darker tips and linear shading whereas the throat is Persian yellow.

4

Jwala

Jwala is a seedling produced by selfing G. psittacines hybrid. The spikes of this variety are branched with a mean spike length of about 65cm. The florets of this variety are Vermilion with sparingly spread long linear streaks of Vermilion. There are around 14-18 florets per spike in this variety.

5

Manhar

This variety of Gladiolus was released by NBRI, Lucknow. It is basically a cross between G. 'Friendship' x G. 'tristis'. The spikes of this variety are one-sided with a mean spike length of about 60cm. The florets of this variety are Primrose Yellow in colour and the tips of outer three petals are splashed with Tyrian Rose. There are around 14-18 florets per spike.

6

Manisha

The Manisha variety is a cross between G. 'Friendship' x G. 'tristis'. The spikes of this variety are one-sided with a mean spike length of around 60cm. The florets of this variety are white with outer three petals splashed with Tyrian Rose colour more towards margins. There are around 14-16 florets per spike in this variety.

7

Manmohan

This variety of Gladiolus is a cross between G. 'Friendship' x G. 'tristis'. The spikes are one-sided with a mean spike length 80cm. The florets of this variety are Primrose Yellow and have irregular splashes of Orchid Purple colour at the tips of outer three petals. There are around 14-16 florets per spike in this variety.

8

Mohini

This variety of Gladiolus was released by NBRI, Lucknow. The spikes of the Mohini variety are one-sided with a mean spike length around 60cm. The florets of this variety are white with outer three and inner upper petal heavily splashed with Tyrian Rose. There are about 12-14 florets per spike found in this variety.

9

Mukta

This variety is a cross between G. 'Friendship' x G. 'tristis'. The spikes of the Mukta variety are one-sided with a mean spike length of around 70cm. The florets of this variety are Sulphur Yellow with splashes of Orchid purple in an irregular fashion. There are about 12-15 loosely arranged florets per spike in this variety.

10

Triloki

The Triloki is an Indian bred late-season hybrid developed by crossing G. 'Friendship' x G. 'tristis'. The spikes of this variety are one-sided with a mean spike length of around 75cm. The florets of Triloki are China Rose in the upper half portion and Primrose Yellow in the lower half. The petals of this variety have splashes of China Rose along the margins.

11

Jacksonville Gold

The Jacksonville Gold is an exotic variety of Gladiolus having a mean spike length of 68cm. The florets of this variety are empire yellow in colour with throat blotched ruby red. There are around 14-16 florets per spike found in this variety.

12

White Goddess

The White Goddess is an exotic variety of Gladiolus and has a branched spike with a mean spike length of about 65cm. The florets of this variety are white in colour with throat blotched violet purple. There are around 17-20 florets per spike.

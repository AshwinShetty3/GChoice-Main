The Land of Tea

Assam is one of the leading states for producing tea & is among best tea exporters from India. In India, Assam has occupied a unique place among all, by producing more than 50 percent of the national production having plantation area of about 3.22 lakh hectares which is almost half of the country’s total area under tea.

Assam means ‘peerless” and that reflects the nature of teas. It is believed by people that ‘Haven’t woken up fully if you haven’t sipped a cup of Assam Tea’. Assam tea region is known for producing both Orthodox as well as CTC (crush, tea, curl) variety of teas. Variety of techniques of processing tea and a number of different forms of tea have developed over the years that are now exotic and exclusive to them of origin. Assam is the only region around the globe, which has its own variety of tea, called camellia assamica, which gives a malty sweetness and earthy flavor. In most households, tea is consumed on a regular basis around the world and has begun a way into our hearts from our tables.

Assam tea leaves come from the state of Assam which happens to be the world’s largest tea growing area. Assam tea and its blends are popular worldwide as breakfast tea. To be more precise, the Irish breakfast tea consists of a blend of black teas including Assam tea leaves. This type of tea is cultivated in the lowlands near the bank of the river Brahmaputra, the main factor leading to its malty taste.

Variants of Tea

  • Black Tea

    It is a type of tea that is more oxidized than oolong, green and white tea leaves. It is generally stronger in flavor. The method of producing tea is very efficient and effective and gives a better-quality product. The flavor of black tea maintains the taste for several years.

  • White Tea

    It is a type of tea which is lightly oxidized. The plant leaves of the tea are harvested before it’s open fully. The young buds are still covered by white hair, hence it got its name as “white” tea.

  • Green Tea

    It is a type of tea which is made from the leaves from Camellia Sinensis. It is really good for health too, and has many benefits for humans.

These three varieties are very common in India. Tea is the most consumed soft beverage after water, in the world.

Benefits of Tea

“Drinking tea has many benefits to keep you healthy”. Tea is one of the most beneficial beverages. It has benefits both for your mind and for your body. It contains antioxidant compounds and may help to boost your heart health too. No matter what season it is, tea, iced or hot is very beneficial for your body. Different types of tea have different benefits. Here are some of the key benefits of Tea for your health:

  • It is great for boosting the immune system. Many studies have shown that a variety of teas may boost your immune system and fight off inflammation. They can also be helpful to ward-off cancer and heart diseases.

  • It helps in weight loss. Teas have a flavonoid called catechins that may boost metabolism and help your body to break down fats quickly. The caffeine in teas may help to increase your energy use causing your body to burn more calories. Thus, tea is useful for weight loss too.

  • It helps to control diabetes. Especially teas such as black tea, green tea, oolong tea and so on are said to be helpful to lower blood sugar levels. These teas contain polyphenols which have antioxidant properties that help to prevent type 2 diabetes.

  • It helps in anti-aging and reduces the risk of cancer. The topical application of green tea extracts along with the consumption of this tea may help improve the elasticity of the skin and therefore slow down the skin ageing process.

  • It helps relieve stress. Some research states that tea helps people to recover more quickly from a stressful job. Tea naturally increases serotonin and melatonin levels in your body making you feel relaxed.

  • It helps to cure coughs and cold. the war,th of a cup of tea may help to soothe your throat that may feel raw and sore from coughing. It may also help to loosen the mucus. Warm fluids like a hot cup of tea can help to break up mucus and thus are helpful during cold and cough.

  • It reduces infections. Green tea has been known to help combat these organisms in various ways directly or even indirectly. Teas also have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that may also help for the antimicrobial effects.

  • It helps to boost energy level. Tea contains a moderate amount of caffeine which may give you the energy you need on a sluggish and boring day. It is also a health alternative rather than drinking other caffeinated beverages.

  • It reduces high blood pressure. Some research studies state that consuming tea may help to lower blood pressure levels too.

  • It is an excellent source of antioxidant. Various teas are rich in antioxidants. Teas are known to be rich in a particular type of antioxidants called flavonoids. These antioxidants have a detoxifying effect and protect the cells from free radicals.
